Butter Chicken

Description

Butter chicken is a creamy and flavorful dish that comes from India. It's made with tender pieces of chicken cooked in a rich and creamy tomato-based sauce. The sauce is flavored with spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala, giving it a deliciously warm and aromatic taste. Butter chicken is often served with naan or rice, making it a comforting and satisfying meal enjoyed by many around the world. Whether you're a fan of Indian cuisine or trying it for the first time, butter chicken is sure to delight your taste buds with its creamy texture and bold flavors.

Ingrediants

  • Chicken: Boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • Butter or ghee: Used for cooking and flavoring the dish.
  • Tomato puree or crushed tomatoes: Provides the base for the creamy sauce.
  • Heavy cream: Adds richness and creaminess to the sauce.
  • Aromatics: Minced garlic and ginger for flavor.
  • Spices: Common spices include cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder.
  • Honey or sugar: Used to balance the acidity of the tomatoes and add a touch of sweetness.
  • Salt: To season the dish.
  • Fresh cilantro: Chopped cilantro for garnishing and added freshness.
  • Optionally, yogurt or cashew paste: Some recipes may include yogurt or ground cashews for added creaminess and flavor.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and a pinch of turmeric. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for best results.
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    1. Heat some butter or ghee in a pan over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    1. In the same pan, add a bit more butter or ghee if needed.
    2. Saut√© minced garlic and ginger until fragrant.
    3. Add tomato puree or crushed tomatoes and cook until the raw flavor is gone and the sauce thickens slightly.
    4. Stir in spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder.
    5. Cook for another minute to toast the spices
  4. Simmer the Sauce:
    1. Reduce the heat to low and stir in heavy cream.
    2. Add honey or sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes.
    3. Let the sauce simmer gently for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken.
  5. Finish the Dish:
    1. Once the sauce has reached your desired consistency, return the cooked chicken pieces to the pan.
    2. Stir well to coat the chicken in the creamy sauce.
    3. Let the chicken simmer in the sauce for a few more minutes to heat through and absorb the flavors.
  6. Serve:
    1. Garnish with chopped cilantro.
    2. Serve hot with rice, naan, or your favorite bread.
